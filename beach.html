BEACHES

With its sun-kissed sands, turquoise waters, secluded coves and vibrant coastal culture, the beaches of God’s Own Country sings a different song to every traveller who seeks its shores.Kerala, the southernmost state of India shares around 600km of shoreline with the Arabian Sea. Thus it hosts some of the fascinating beaches in India. The shoreline topography is peculiar and changes promptly as one proceeds from north to south. Places such as Bekal, Kannur, Muzhapilangad, and Thalassery in the north Kerala exhibit rise of headlands above the shore from the marginal of the beaches. Beaches from Kozhikode to the south of Kerala display flat lands with rocky extensions projecting out. However, Kerala is known for its serene beauty of coconut trees-lined beaches, rivers, backwaters, and a network of canals throughout its terrain.


LET'S EXPLORE BEACHES IN KERALA
Kovalam Beach

Kovalam Beach is well-known for its diverse leisure options. Sunbathing, swimming, ayurvedic body massages, and occasional cultural programs are a number of them. The beach is all on late in the day and moves on well into the night. They house the budget cottages, Ayurvedic health resorts, convention facilities, shopping zones, swimming pools, Yoga, and herbal massage centers. One of the soundest features to enjoy on the beach is its Catamaran cruising. A traditional wooden catamaran boat that rows using bamboo paddles is the best choice to explore the most out of it. Also, the sunset cruises the beach offers to its guests are worth talking about then.

Varkala Beach

Varkala Beach (also called Papanasham Beach), is located in Varkala town lies in the suburbs of Thiruvananthapuram district. At Varkala, one gets to experience the life of a balmy and quiet settlement and enjoy the beauty of marine life. Many Ayurveda centers have been established, on the sides of these cliffs, which adds to its charm. One of the main attractions to this place all year along is a 2000-year-old Janardhanaswamy Temple and the Ashramam-Sivagiri Mutt.


Cherai Beach

If you are planning for a side trip from Kochi, Cherai is the best option. This beach is one of the most visited beaches in Kerala which is located on Vypin Island. Reaching this attractive beach is quite a captivating ride through villages and paddy fields. The beach is situated at an isolated location to a large extent which explains why it’s one of the quietest beaches one can visit in Kerala. The beach is ideal for swimmers as the tide is substantially low and the waves are calm. There’s an opportunity for frequent dolphin sightings. It is a rare sight that the Cherai beach comes with the backwaters and the sea in a single frame. Thus, it attracts visitors and is undoubtedly one of the best beaches in Kerala.

Kannur Beach

The Payyambalam Beach is situated close to Kannur city, filled with white sand and gentle waters. Also, a prominent geographical feature it offers is flat laterite cliffs, with a portion of which stick into the sea. The beach is a good hotspot for couples and families looking to chill, relax and unwind and is surrounded by beach resorts and homestays. For those who seek a little adventure, options like surfing, speed boat, and swimming are also available. The hotels nearby provide an incredible view of the beach to guests, especially the sunset view.


Bekal Beach

At the west coast of Kerala in the northernmost district, Kasaragod lies the massive Bekal Fort; This is the largest and impeccably preserved fort in the whole of Kerala, bordered by a beach. Sitting on 35km headland, 130 feet above sea level, the Bekal Beach provides an imposing view. One of the general things to do as a guest at Bekal is getting onto the keyhole-shaped historic fort and enjoying the sight of the Arabian Sea from its tall observation towers. The grand architecture on laterite slabs makes the Bekal fort the most visited beach destination in Kerala.

Kappil Beach

As one of Kerala's finest and cleanest beaches, Kappil invites you to unwind and reconnect with yourself. Picture yourself walking along the shore, the soft sands beneath your feet, and the gentle sea breeze kissing your skin. The horizon stretches endlessly, inviting contemplation and offering a sanctuary for introspection.Away from the crowds, Kappil Beach offers a sanctuary where you can confront your thoughts in peace, enveloped by the soothing sounds of waves lapping against the shore. It's a place where time slows down, allowing you to savour each moment and rejuvenate your spirit amidst nature's unspoiled beauty.


Chavakkad Beach

Chavakkad Beach is renowned for its unspoiled beauty and peaceful surroundings. The beach stretches along the Arabian Sea, providing visitors with stunning views and a soothing environment. The beach features golden sands and blue waters of the Arabian Sea. The pristine shoreline creates a perfect setting for sunbathing, swimming, and leisurely walks along the shore. One of the highlights of Chavakkad Beach is its incredible sunsets. As the sun dips below the horizon, the sky is painted with vibrant hues, creating a mesmerizing and dreamy backdrop.

Shankhumugham Beach

Shankhumugham Beach in Thiruvananthapuram is a beloved spot for both locals and visitors alike, offering a perfect blend of relaxation and scenic beauty. Renowned for its tranquil vibe, many people visit the beach to witness its stunning sunsets, while others find solace during the day amidst the gentle breeze and the expansive view of the Arabian Sea. The beach's rustic charm is further accentuated by the presence of local fishermen and several picturesque vantage points that make it an ideal spot for picnics.One of the beach's most iconic features is the Matsya Kanyaka sculpture, a 35-meter-long mermaid structure that stands as a unique and impressive landmark in the state.


Muzhapilangad Beach

Muzhappilangad Beach is a unique coastal retreat offering the thrill of adventure and the serenity of nature, located just 7 km from Thalassery in Kannur, Kerala. Spanning 4 km of sandy shores along the enchanting Malabar Coast, it is the longest drive-in beach in Asia, allowing visitors to experience the joy of driving along the beach, whether on a motorbike or in a car, with the wind in their hair.The beach's iconic black rocks shield it from strong ocean currents, creating a swimmer’s paradise with calm, safe waters. These rocks also serve as a haven for migratory birds, making Muzhappilangad a hotspot for birdwatching, especially during winter when over 30 species of birds, including terns, gulls, and sandpipers, flock to its shores.

Kappad Beach

Located in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, Kappad Beach stands out for its historical importance and serene beauty. Known as the landing site of Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama on May 20, 1498, this beach marked the start of European trade in India. With its golden sands, calm waters, and cultural significance, Kappad offers a unique blend of history, biodiversity, and scenic charm.Kappad Beach is forever etched in history as the spot where Vasco da Gama arrived with his fleet, opening the first sea route from Europe to India. This event was pivotal in shaping Indian history, heralding an era of European trade and colonization. A commemorative stone monument stands on the beach, drawing history enthusiasts and visitors from across the globe.
